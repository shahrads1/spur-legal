Spur — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 23, 2026
Last updated: July 23, 2026

Spur ("Spur", "we", "us") is operated by Spur Social Corp., a corporation incorporated under the Canada Business Corporations Act with its registered office at 2303-50 O'Neill Rd, North York, Ontario M3C 0R1, Canada. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect through the Spur mobile app and related services (the "Service"), why we collect it, how we use, share, and protect it, how long we keep it, and the rights and choices you have — including under Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable U.S. state privacy laws.

By using the Service, you acknowledge this Policy. Where the law requires consent for a particular collection or use, we will ask for it at the relevant moment (for example, device permissions for location and notifications).

1. Who can use Spur

The Service is for people 18 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If we learn that we have, we will delete it and terminate the account. If you believe someone under 18 is using Spur, report it in-app or email shahrads@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

2a. Information you give us

  • Phone number — used to create and verify your account with a one-time SMS passcode.
  • Profile information — your first name, optional bio, profile photo, and your activity interests.
  • Third-party sign-in data — if you sign in with a supported provider (such as Google or Apple), we receive the basic information that provider shares (such as name and email), as controlled by your settings with that provider.
  • Location you permit — see Section 4. We never store your precise GPS position; coordinates are blurred to roughly 1 km before storage.
  • Payment information — when an Event requires a deposit, your card is collected and processed entirely by Stripe. We never see or store your full card number. We store a Stripe customer reference and a card "fingerprint" (a token Stripe derives from the card) used only for fraud and abuse prevention.
  • Identity verification — if you use optional ID verification, it is processed by Stripe Identity. We receive only the result (pass/fail and verification level), not your ID document or its contents.
  • Content you create — events, posts, messages, reviews, RSVPs, reports, and feedback.
  • Communications with us — support requests, appeals, and correspondence.

2b. Information collected automatically

  • Usage data — how you interact with the app (screens viewed, features used, RSVPs, check-ins), used to operate and improve the Service.
  • Device data — device type, operating system, app version, language, time zone, IP address, and a push-notification token if you enable notifications.
  • Check-in location — when you check in to an Event, your device reports its location once, used solely to confirm you are near the venue at that moment.
  • Log and diagnostic data — error reports and performance data used to keep the Service working.

2c. Information from other users and sources

  • Reports and reviews — other users may submit content about you (for example, a safety report or an event review). We treat reports confidentially and use them for moderation and safety.
  • Venues and partners — participating venues may confirm attendance or booking details related to Events.

We do not request or knowingly collect sensitive categories of information such as health data, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, or biometric identifiers. Please do not include sensitive information in your profile or posts.

4. Location — how we protect it

Location powers Spur's core experience (nearby plans, check-in), so we designed it conservatively:

  • Your exact location is never stored and never shown to other users. Coordinates are rounded/blurred to approximately 1 km on our servers before saving, so other users only ever see your rough area.
  • Check-in uses your device's reported location once, solely to confirm you are near the venue at the event time.
  • You control location permissions in your device settings. Disabling location limits features (discovery and check-in) but you can still use the app.

6. Data retention and account deletion

  • Active accounts: we keep your information while your account exists and as needed to provide the Service.
  • Deletion: you can delete your account in-app at any time. When you do, we delete or de-identify your profile and personal content within a reasonable period, except as described below.
  • Limited retention after deletion: we may retain (i) records we are legally required to keep (for example, payment/tax records, kept per statutory periods); (ii) information reasonably needed to resolve disputes, enforce our agreements, or investigate violations, kept only as long as needed for that purpose; and (iii) the safety tombstone described next.
  • Safety "tombstone": if an account is banned for serious violations, we retain a limited, irreversible record — a one-way cryptographic hash of the phone number and card fingerprint — used solely to prevent the banned person from re-registering. It cannot be reversed into your phone number or card details, is never shown to anyone, and is never used for marketing.
  • Messages: messages you send exist in the recipient's copy of the conversation; deleting your account removes your profile from those conversations.

7. Your communication choices (CASL / TCPA)

  • Transactional messages (verification codes, event confirmations, safety notices) are part of the Service and are sent as needed.
  • Marketing or promotional messages (including any future "Promotions" features) will only be sent with your consent, in compliance with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL) and, for U.S. users, the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA). Every marketing message will include a way to unsubscribe, and you can withdraw consent at any time in settings or by following the unsubscribe mechanism. Consent to marketing is never a condition of using the Service.
  • Push notifications can be turned on or off in your device settings at any time.
  • Standard carrier message and data rates may apply to SMS.

8. Cookies and analytics

The Spur app does not use advertising cookies or third-party ad trackers. Our website may use strictly necessary cookies and basic, privacy-respecting analytics to understand aggregate traffic. We honour browser Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where applicable law requires it — though note we do not sell or share personal information in any case.

9. Security

We protect personal information with safeguards appropriate to its sensitivity, including encryption in transit (TLS), encryption at rest with our infrastructure providers, hashed secrets, role-based access controls, row-level security on our database, and provider-side handling of all card data (we are never in possession of full card numbers). No system is perfectly secure; if we discover a breach of security safeguards creating a real risk of significant harm, we will notify affected users and the appropriate regulators as required by law (including PIPEDA's breach-reporting rules and applicable U.S. state breach-notification laws).

10. International data transfers

We are based in Canada, and our service providers (listed in Section 5) process data in the United States and possibly other countries. When your information is processed outside your province, state, or country, it is subject to the laws of those jurisdictions, and courts, law enforcement, and national-security authorities there may be able to access it under their laws. We use contractual and technical safeguards with our providers to protect information comparably wherever it is processed. By using the Service, you acknowledge this cross-border processing.

11. Your rights — Canada

Under PIPEDA (and substantially similar provincial laws, including Quebec's Law 25), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you and ask how it has been used and disclosed;
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Withdraw consent to collection, use, or disclosure (subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice — withdrawing consent for essential processing may mean we cannot provide the Service);
  • Delete your account and personal information as described in Section 6; and
  • Complain — first to us, and if you are not satisfied, to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (priv.gc.ca) or your provincial privacy regulator (in Quebec, the Commission d'accès à l'information).

To exercise any right, email shahrads@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days (or the period required by your applicable law), and we may need to verify your identity first. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

Depending on your state (including California, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas, Utah, and Virginia, among others), you may have the right to: know/access the personal information we have collected about you; correct inaccuracies; delete your personal information; receive a portable copy; and opt out of "sales," "sharing"/targeted advertising, and certain profiling. Spur does not sell personal information and does not share it for targeted advertising, and we do not engage in profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

For reference, the categories of personal information we collect (as described in Section 2) are: identifiers (name, phone number, device identifiers, IP address); customer records (payment references via Stripe); approximate (coarse) geolocation; internet/app activity; audio-visual content you upload (photos); and inferences limited to interests and reliability scoring within the app. We collect them from you, your device, other users, and our providers, for the purposes in Section 3, and disclose them to the recipients in Section 5. We retain them as described in Section 6.

To exercise a right, email shahrads@gmail.com with your request and the phone number on your account. You may use an authorized agent where your state's law allows; we will verify the request. If we decline a request, you may appeal by replying to our decision, and we will review the appeal per your state's requirements. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. California residents: we do not sell or share personal information as defined by the CCPA/CPRA, and we have not done so in the preceding 12 months; we also do not use or disclose sensitive personal information for purposes requiring a right to limit.

13. Do Not Track

Some browsers send "Do Not Track" signals. Because there is no common standard, our website does not respond to DNT signals, but we honour GPC as noted in Section 8, and in any event we do not track users across third-party sites or sell personal information.
