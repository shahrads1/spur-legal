Spur — Community Guidelines

Effective date: July 23, 2026

Spur exists to help people beat loneliness by doing real things together — a pickup game, a pub table, a hike, a night out. That only works if everyone feels safe, welcome, and respected, online and in person. These Guidelines apply to everything you do on Spur: your profile, events, posts, messages, reviews — and your behaviour at events arranged through Spur. They form part of our Terms of Service. Breaking them can get content removed, features restricted, deposits forfeited, and your account suspended or permanently banned.

The short version

  • Be real. One account, your real self, honest events.
  • Be respectful. Treat people the way you'd want to be treated at a table of strangers.
  • Show up. RSVP means something here. Come, or cancel early.
  • Keep everyone safe. Report what's wrong. Never make anyone feel unsafe.

1. Authenticity

  • Be yourself. Use your real first name and a photo that's actually you. No impersonating any person, brand, or organization.
  • One person, one account. Don't create multiple accounts, share accounts, or create an account for someone else — and don't come back on a new account after a ban.
  • Honest events. Describe your plans accurately: what, where, when, who it's for, and any costs. Bait-and-switch events (advertising one thing, running another) are banned.
  • No deception. Catfishing, fake profiles, misleading photos, and misrepresenting your age or identity — including to access restricted events — are prohibited.

2. Respect

  • No harassment or bullying. No insults, intimidation, humiliation, unwanted sexual attention, or piling on — online or at events.
  • No hate. No content or conduct that attacks, demeans, or excludes people based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, immigration status, or any other protected characteristic. Slurs, hateful symbols, and "jokes" built on hate count.
  • No threats or violence. No threatening, promoting, glorifying, or engaging in violence, and no content supporting violent extremism, terrorism, or organized hate.
  • Respect "no." If someone blocks you, declines your request, or asks you to stop contacting them — stop. Attempting to reach them through other events, accounts, or channels is harassment.

3. Not a hookup app — and never for minors

  • Spur is for activities and friendship, not dating or hookups. Don't use it to solicit sex, send sexual content, or make events or messages sexual. Nudity and sexually explicit content are prohibited.
  • Zero tolerance for anything involving minors. Spur is 18+. Any sexual content involving a minor, any attempt to interact with a minor, and any account belonging to someone under 18 will be removed, permanently banned, and reported to authorities where appropriate.

4. Keep it legal and safe

  • No illegal activity, and no organizing it — including drug sales, weapons, stolen goods, or events built around unlawful conduct.
  • No events or content that endanger people: dangerous stunts, unsafe venues, encouraging excessive drinking, or activities without regard for participants' safety.
  • No promotion of self-harm, suicide, or eating disorders. If you or someone on Spur is struggling, see our Safety Guidelines for crisis resources.

5. No scams, spam, or selling

  • Never ask other users for money, financial information, passwords, or investments. Romance scams, crypto pitches, and "emergencies" that need a wire transfer get you banned and reported.
  • No spam, chain messages, mass invites, or repetitive posting.
  • No advertising, promotion, recruiting, MLM, or commercial solicitation through profiles, events, or messages — unless you're an approved venue partner acting through approved features. Hosting a genuine event at your favourite spot is great; using events as a sales funnel is not.

6. Privacy of others

  • Don't share anyone's private information — phone number, address, workplace, photos, messages — without their consent ("doxxing").
  • Don't photograph or record people at events in ways they haven't agreed to, and take it down if someone asks.
  • What happens at an event stays within the group's comfort level. Attendee lists and conversations aren't content for public posting without consent.

7. Show up — reliability matters

  • If you RSVP and get approved, come — or cancel as early as you can. Plans on Spur only work when people are reliable. Repeated no-shows hurt hosts, venues, and other guests.
  • No-shows can forfeit your deposit (for deposit events), lower your reliability score, and lead to restrictions or suspension.
  • Hosts: don't cancel confirmed events casually, don't approve more people than the plan can hold, and communicate changes promptly. Host reliability is tracked too.

8. Hosting standards

  • Only host events you genuinely intend to run, at real places, at real times.
  • Be clear about costs. Guests pay venues directly for what they order; hosts must not collect money from guests through or around Spur.
  • Follow venue rules and applicable laws (capacity, liquor laws, noise, permits where needed).
  • Set the tone: welcome people, make introductions, and address bad behaviour — including reporting it to us.

9. Women-only and restricted events

Spur offers women-only events (and may offer other bona fide restricted event types) as a safety and comfort feature. These restrictions are enforced. Misrepresenting yourself to access a restricted event is a serious violation.

10. Reviews and reports

  • Reviews must reflect your genuine experience. No fake, retaliatory, or extortionate reviews.
  • Reports exist to keep people safe. Knowingly false reports are themselves a violation.

11. Safety tools

  • Report any user, event, post, message, or review — in-app or via shahrads@gmail.com. We review reports and aim to act on serious safety issues within 24 hours.
  • Block anyone you don't want to interact with — they disappear from your feed and messages, both ways, immediately.
  • In an emergency, always contact 911 (or your local emergency number) first.

12. Enforcement

When we find a violation — through reports, moderation, or our own review — we act based on severity, context, and history. Actions include: a warning; removing content; restricting features (like hosting or messaging); forfeiting event deposits where the violation relates to attendance abuse; temporary suspension; or permanent ban. Serious violations — violence, threats, sexual misconduct, anything involving minors, fraud, or endangering others — result in an immediate permanent ban, and a tombstone record prevents re-registration (see our Privacy Policy). Where appropriate, we report conduct to law enforcement.

13. Appeals

If you believe we made a mistake, you can appeal by emailing shahrads@gmail.com from the phone-verified account (or including your account phone number), within 30 days of the action, explaining why. We'll review in good faith and reply with the outcome. Appeals of permanent bans for serious safety violations are rarely granted.

14. These Guidelines evolve

Communities change, and so will these Guidelines. Material updates will be announced in-app. The examples above are illustrative, not exhaustive — we may act on conduct that violates the spirit of these rules even if it isn't listed word-for-word.

Note: these Guidelines also satisfy app-store requirements for apps with user-generated content — a published code of conduct, in-app reporting and blocking, and a commitment to act on objectionable-content reports within 24 hours.
